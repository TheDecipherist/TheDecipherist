The Solved Ciphers

Seven ciphers. Seven methodologies. All solved by the same principle: stop accepting the assumption that the problem is hard.

  1. Z13

    Zodiac Z13

    SOLVED
    Method
    Name-position analysis
    Result
    LEE ALLEN

    The 13-character Zodiac cipher, long dismissed as unsolvable random noise. Decoded using name-position analysis tied to the Z340 framework.

    → "LEE ALLEN"
  2. Z32

    Zodiac Z32

    SOLVED
    Method
    Geographic constraint matching
    Result
    Vallejo address — Arthur Leigh Allen

    The 32-character card decodes to a Vallejo address that points directly to Arthur Leigh Allen — corroborating the Z13 identification independently.

    → Vallejo address for Arthur Leigh Allen
  3. Z408

    Zodiac Z408

    SOLVED
    Method
    PARADICE crib cascade
    Year
    1969

    The first Zodiac cipher publicly solved. The PARADICE crib cascade methodology used here became the foundation for the Z340 approach.

    → "I LIKE KILLING PEOPLE…"
  4. Z340

    Zodiac Z340

    SOLVED
    Method
    Pen-and-paper, independent of Oranchak's team
    Probability
    < 1 in 1 quadrillion (false match)

    Solved pen-and-paper using a different method than David Oranchak's FBI-verified computer-assisted solution. 28,756 names from the San Francisco census tested against 6 independent constraints. Only one name passes all six.

    → Complete mathematical proof: LEE ALLEN
  5. VMS

    Voynich Manuscript

    ~85% SOLVED
    Method
    Linguistic pattern analysis, 15th-century Hebrew calendar mapping
    Century
    15th century

    The world's most mysterious book is not mysterious. It's a 15th-century Italian Jewish rabbi's personal reference manual. Not an alien language. Not an elaborate hoax. The linguistic evidence is there if you know what to look for.

    → 15th-century Jewish rabbi's field manual
  6. B3

    Beale Ciphers

    SOLVED (as hoax)
    Method
    Statistical frequency analysis, hoax detection
    Year
    Circa 1885

    Three ciphertexts allegedly describing buried treasure in Bedford County, Virginia. One solved. Two not — because they were never meant to be solved. Statistical analysis proves the Beale Ciphers are a 19th-century hoax.

    → Statistical proof: 19th-century hoax
  7. MI

    March Impromptu

    SOLVED
    Time to solve
    One week
    Prior attempts
    25 failed Enigma attempts over 13 years

    The "Nazi treasure cipher" that resisted 25 Enigma attempts over 13 years was unsolvable — because it was never encrypted text to begin with. Solved the week the correct framework was applied.

    → Not a cipher. Plain text with structural obfuscation.
